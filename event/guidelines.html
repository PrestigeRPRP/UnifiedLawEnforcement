About General Standard Operation Procedure

This document outlines the guidelines and etiquette for radio communication within the State of San Andreas Law Enforcement Department. Its purpose is to ensure efficient, effective, and respectful communication among officers and dispatchers, ultimately contributing to public safety and mission success.

Description of Content


• Codes and Signals: Standardized codes and signals for emergency and non-emergency transmissions.
• Digital Patrol Message (DPM) Guidelines: Metrics for evaluating dispatcher performance and consequences for non-compliance
• Rules and Regulations: Guidelines for clear, concise, and secure radio communication, as well as prohibitions against inappropriate behavior and misuse of the radio system.



Key Points

1. Adhere to Codes and Signals:
Follow the established codes and signals to ensure efficient communication.

2. Prioritize Clear and Concise Communication:
Speak clearly, use plain language, and be concise in your transmissions.

3. Respectful Communication:
Avoid using offensive language, making personal attacks, or engaging in unnecessary arguments.

4. Proper Use of the Radio System:
Use the radio system only for official business and avoid unnecessary chatter.

5. Follow Dispatch Instructions:
Adhere to the instructions and guidance provided by the dispatcher.

6. Maintain Radio Etiquette:
Be mindful of others and avoid actions that may disrupt communication or cause annoyance.

By following these guidelines, officers and dispatchers can contribute to a more effective and professional law enforcement agency.

Digital Patrol Message (DPM) Guidelines



• Purpose:
The DPM system is intended for urgent or time-sensitive messages that require immediate attention from all units.

• Proper Usage:
1. Use clear and concise language.
2. Provide essential information, such as location, incident type, and suspect description.
3. Avoid using the DPM system for routine or non-urgent messages.

• Improper Usage:
1. Misusing the DPM system can lead to disciplinary action.
2. Avoid spamming the system with unnecessary messages.

Consequences for Violations

Failure to comply with these rules and regulations may result in disciplinary action, including:
• Verbal warnings
• Written reprimands
• Demerits
• Suspension
• Termination

General Guidelines

1. Clear and Concise Communication:

• Clarity: Speak clearly and enunciate words distinctly.
• Simplicity: Keep messages concise and to the point.
• Brevity: Avoid unnecessary details.


2. Proper Use of Radio Terms and Codes:

• Utilize standardized radio codes and terms to expedite communication.
• Avoid using slang or jargon that may not be understood by all listeners.


3. Active Listening:

• Pay close attention to all radio transmissions.
• Avoid interrupting others.
• Ask clarifying questions if necessary.


4. Timely Responses:

• Respond promptly to radio calls and dispatches.
• Avoid unnecessary delays.
