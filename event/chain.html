Chain of Command

About Chain of Command

Los Santos Police Department is a highly structured organization that operates on a strict chain of command. This hierarchy ensures efficient communication, accountability, and the effective execution of duties.

Chain of Command Structures the following:

      Chief of Police
      Assistant Chief
      Deputy Chief
      Commander
      Captain
      Lieutenant
      Sergeant II/Detective III
      Sergeant/Detective II

Details

  • Chief of Police: The highest-ranking officer in the LSPD, responsible for overall leadership, policy implementation, and strategic planning.
  • Assistant Chief of Police: Oversees and report all operational activity within the department, such as patrol, investigations, or administrative services to the Chief of Police.
  • Deputy Chief of Police: Directly report to the Assistant the Chief of Police in managing the department's operations and serves as a liaison between the department and other city agencies.
  • Commander: Heads a major unit or bureau within the department, such as the Patrol Division, Detective Bureau, or Support Services Bureau.
  • Captain: Supervises a specific precinct or specialized unit within a division.
  • Lieutenant: Commands a platoon or shift within a precinct or specialized unit.
  • Sergeant II/Detective III: Supervises a squad of officers and is responsible for daily operations and tactical assignments.
  • Sergeant/Detective II: Assists Sergeants II/Detective III in supervising squads and may specialize in detective work.
