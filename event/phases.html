Field Training

Foundation For Your Career

About

A Field Training Program is a structured process designed to equip new police officers with the necessary skills, knowledge, and confidence to effectively perform their duties. The program typically consists of four phases.

Phase 1: Introduction Phase

During this initial phase, new officers observe experienced officers in action, learning about department policies, procedures, and expectations. They familiarize themselves with the basics of police work, including patrol techniques, traffic stops, and interactions with the public.

Phase 2: Evaluation Phase

Under the direct supervision of a Field Training Officer (FTO), new officers begin to perform basic police duties. The FTO demonstrates the correct techniques and provides guidance and feedback. This phase helps officers develop practical skills and build confidence.

Phase 3: Plain Clothes Phase

In this phase, new officers practice performing police duties independently, with the FTO providing support and supervision as needed. Officers gradually take on more responsibility and begin to make decisions on their own. This phase allows officers to apply their learned skills in real-world scenarios.

Phase 4: Final Evaluation Phase

During this initial phase, new officers observe experienced officers in action, learning about department policies, procedures, and expectations. They familiarize themselves with the basics of police work, including patrol techniques, traffic stops, and interactions with the public.

Steps for Interns to Contact or Ask FTO for Phases

    Understand the Program Structure

Familiarize yourself with the overall structure of the Field Training Program and the specific phases involved. This will help you understand the progression and expectations of each phase.

    Identify Your FTO

Know who your assigned Field Training Officer (FTO) is and how to contact them. This information should be provided to you upon starting the program

    Prepare Your Questions

Before contacting your FTO, prepare a list of questions you want to ask. This will help you stay focused and ensure you get the information you need.

    Schedule a Meeting

Request a meeting with your FTO to discuss your progress and ask your questions. This could be a face-to-face meeting or a virtual meeting, depending on your department's policies.

    Be Proactive

Don't hesitate to reach out to your FTO if you have concerns or questions. The goal of the FTP is to support your growth and development, so it's important to communicate openly and honestly.

    Follow Up

After your meeting, follow up with your FTO to ensure you understand their feedback and any next steps. This will help you stay on track and make the most of the program.
