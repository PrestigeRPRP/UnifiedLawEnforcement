Type of Searches

Ideate. Strategize. Execute.

About


This document outlines the various types of searches conducted by law enforcement and the exceptions to the search warrant requirement. It provides a brief overview of each type of search, including plain view searches, protective searches, consent searches, searches incident to arrest, and search warrants. It also discusses the specific circumstances under which a search warrant may be waived, such as exigent circumstances, hot pursuit, and the automobile exception.

Types of Searches

    Plain View Search:
    • Observation of evidence or contraband without a search.
    • The officer must have a lawful right to be in the location.

    Protective Search (Pat-Down or Frisk):
    • Conducted for officer safety to ensure against threats.
    • Based on reasonable suspicion that the individual is armed and dangerous.

    Consent Search:
    • Warrantless search conducted with voluntary consent.
    • Consent must be freely and intelligently given.

    Search Incident to Arrest:
    • Warrantless search of the arrestee and immediate area.
    • Limited to preventing harm, escape, or destruction of evidence.

    Search Warrant:
    • Court order authorizing a search based on probable cause.
    • Requires specific information about the place to be searched and items to be seized.


Exceptions to the Search Warrant Requirements

    Protective Search:
    • Reasonable suspicion of a weapon.

    Search Incident to Arrest:
    • Lawful arrest.

    Plain View:
    • Contraband or illegal activity in plain sight.

    Consent:
    • Voluntary consent from a person with authority.

    Hot Pursuit:
    • Pursuit of a fleeing suspect into a residence.

    Exigent Circumstances:
    • Emergency situations, danger to life, or evidence destruction.

    Automobile Exception:
    • Probable cause to believe a vehicle contains contraband.
